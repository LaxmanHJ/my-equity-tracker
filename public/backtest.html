How Backtesting Works

Backtesting is a way to prove whether a trading idea actually makes money before you risk any real cash on it.

🤔

1. The "What If"

Imagine it is January 1st, 2023. Our script looks at the data available only up to that day and checks our technical conditions (e.g., Markov Regime is Bullish, RSI < 30). It signals: Buy INFY.

🖱️

2. The Simulation

The backtesting engine simulates you actually clicking "Buy" on your broker. It records the exact price and subtracts a realistic amount for broker fees (commissions) and bad fill prices (slippage).

⏩

3. The Test

It then fast-forwards time, watching the price of INFY go up and down over the following days and months. Later (e.g., June 15th, 2023), your script evaluates new data and says: "Market is Bearish. Sell."

📊

4. The Result

The engine simulates selling your shares, calculates your exact profit or loss on that specific trade, and updates your total bankroll. It repeats this for every day in the sequence.
