Momentum combines the 1-month (20%), 3-month (40%), and 6-month (40%) returns. It proves that an object in motion stays in motion. A high score means the stock is in a confirmed, mathematically-backed uptrend. A negative score means it's a falling knife.

MEAN REVERSION (Z-SCORE)
-2.50
< -2 (Oversold) 0 (Average) > +2 (Overbought)
Due for bounce

Calculates how many standard deviations the price is away from its 50-day average. A Z-Score below -2.0 means the stock has crashed extraordinarily fast and is historically due for a violent snap-back relief rally. A Z-score above +2.0 means it is dangerously extended.

RELATIVE STRENGTH INDEX (RSI)
75.4
0 (Oversold) 50 (Nuetral) 100 (Overbought)
Overbought

Measures the speed of price movements (0 to 100). An RSI below 30 implies sellers are completely exhausted and a reversal is imminent. An RSI above 70 shows extreme euphoria where all buyers are trapped at the top.

MACD (MOMENTUM ACCELERATION)
Bullish Cross
Bearish Divergence No Trend Bullish Convergence
Trend Reversal

Tracks the difference between the 12-day and 26-day EMA. A Bullish Crossover (when the fast line crosses above the slow line) is the engine's highest-conviction signal that a new, sustainable uptrend has officially begun.

VOLATILITY REGIME
0.85
< 0.8 (Contracting) 1.0 (Stable) > 1.2 (Expanding)
Contracting Volatility

Divides short-term volatility by long-term volatility. A ratio below 1.0 means the stock's price swings are calming down (contracting), creating a secure, low-risk base from which powerful breakouts occur.

Measures pure Alpha. If NIFTY 50 goes up 5% but your stock goes up 17.5%, the stock generated +12.5% excess return. High relative strength proves institutional money is accumulating this specific asset regardless of broader market conditions.

MARKOV CHAIN REGIME DETECTION
Bull Regime
Bear (< -0.5%) Sideways (Flat) Bull (> +0.5%)
Trend Expected

An Index Analysis model that predicts the broader market trend. If the index is stuck in a "Sideways" regime, expect individual stock breakouts to fail entirely. If it shifts to "Bull," trend-following strategies have a highly elevated probability of success.
